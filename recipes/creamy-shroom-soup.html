A Creamy Mushroom Soup

Non-Dairy

This creamy vegan soup is soon to be one of your go-to comfort soups for the cold weather. It's hearty, healthy, and delicious.

I make this soup the second a hint of fall appears and it brings me so much joy.

Ingredients
  • 3/4 cup raw cashews, soaked overnight
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 16 oz cremini mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 yellow onion, diced
  • 2 stalks celery, diced
  • 2 medium carrots, diced
  • 5 cloves garlic
  • 3 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 3 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 1 tsb. flour
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 3/4 cup wild rice blend
  • 5-6 cups vegetable broth
    • instructions
    1. Blend cashews with the water they soaked in until velvety smooth.
    2. Heat olive oil in a large stock pot. Add in the celery, carrots, and onion and sauté over medium heat. Once soft, 8-10 minutes, add the garlic and sauté for another minute. If you have cooking twine, wrap the thyme and rosemary sprigs together. If not, no worries it's just for ease of the stem removal later on. Add the thyme and rosemary to the stock pot.
    3. Add the mushrooms and cook until medium brown and most of the liquids has been released, 12-15 minutes.
    4. Sprinkly the mushrooms with flour and stir continuously for 2-3 minutes. Add the white wine and stir for another few minutes. Move on once thickened and bubbling.
    5. Add the wild rice and broth to the stock pot. Allow to cook for 45 minutes or until the ild rice starts to split open.
    6. Stir in the cashew cream and remove them stems of the thyme and rosemary. Season the soup to tase with salt and pepper. optional: I like to add cumin and garam masala at this step to counter balance the cashew flavor.
    7. Serve up your soup and enjoy! This can be stored for up to 5 days in the fridge or frozen for months.
